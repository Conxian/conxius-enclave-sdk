## Summary

- Carries typed value-bearing settlement authorization from canonical intent through operation binding, digest, algorithm, operation-key identity, verified signature, attestation/provenance, policy identity, and manager-issued replay authorization.
- Revalidates the typed envelope before dispatch and sends the authorization envelope through Opportunity plus the `bisq`, `boltz`, `changelly`, `ntt`, `wormhole`, and `x402` adapters.
- Preserves the signer-identity evidence and fail-closed provider boundary from [PR #216](https://github.com/Conxian/conxius-enclave-sdk/pull/216), building on the containment baseline in [PR #205](https://github.com/Conxian/conxius-enclave-sdk/pull/205).
- Refreshes capability evidence and public readiness documentation with the final reviewed code ref.

## Threat boundary

- Raw signature settlement remains rejected outside isolated test fixtures; production value-bearing callers do not route through `EnclaveManager::sign`.
- The default provider remains unavailable, software drivers remain test/development-only, and WASM development constructors remain explicitly feature-gated.
- Replay protection is process-local containment only. No distributed replay coordination, real hardware/provider verifier, vendor roots/collateral, provider-backed runtime evidence, independent review, or exact release artifacts are added.
