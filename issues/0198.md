# [P0] Make CCTP, account abstraction, and asset metadata fail closed

**Issue #198** | OPEN | Created: 2026-07-20 | Updated: 2026-07-20

CCTP, account-abstraction, asset-metadata, and rail-address placeholders can permit incorrect encoding, routing, or authorization if they are accepted as real configuration. The audit therefore treats these paths as production blockers until they validate canonical data or fail closed.

Refs #191

## Scope
- Harden `src/protocol/cctp.rs` and `src/protocol/account_abstraction.rs` so unsupported or incomplete adapters cannot execute as if they were canonical integrations.
- Harden `src/protocol/asset.rs` and rail address fixtures with chain-specific encoding, network, provenance, and checksum validation.
- Remove or quarantine placeholder and missing addresses from active registries and routing decisions.
- Ensure address and asset metadata errors are typed, deterministic, and non-panicking.

## Evidence
- [Audit PR #193](https://github.com/Conxian/conxius-enclave-sdk/pull/193) records asset metadata, address fixtures, and high-impact adapter gaps as production-enablement blockers.
- Relevant implementation paths: `src/protocol/cctp.rs`, `src/protocol/account_abstraction.rs`, `src/protocol/asset.rs`, and rail address fixtures.
- Open issue #179 and merged [PR #189](https://github.com/Conxian/conxius-enclave-sdk/pull/189) cover BIP-110 limits, not the canonical CCTP, account-abstraction, or address-provenance requirements here.

## Acceptance criteria
- Each enabled adapter has canonical encoding and verification evidence, or is explicitly disabled with a typed unsupported result.
- Address and asset metadata checks are chain- and network-specific and validate provenance, format, checksum, and applicable contract/account constraints.
- Placeholder, missing, malformed, or unverified addresses cannot become active routing or settlement configuration.
- Negative tests cover wrong network, wrong asset, invalid checksum/encoding, missing metadata, placeholder values, and unsupported adapters.
- The registry and support matrix identify the exact active scope without implying support for unverified chains or adapters.

## Dependencies
- Coordinates with canonical verification in #196 and protocol placeholder quarantine in #197.
- Must be complete before the final release-acceptance gate for any affected asset or rail.
